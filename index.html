How it was built

From capstone to game

The data. Four datasets — historical World Cups, every World Cup match, player records, and an Elo rating series spanning 1901–2026. Cleaning and EDA were done in a Jupyter notebook (pandas); this build embeds the cleaned per-team ratings for the real 48 qualifiers and their actual group draw.

The algorithm. Group tables come from a round-robin simulation (Elo win/draw/goal model); the knockout uses single-draw resolution; title odds come from a Monte Carlo over the full bracket. Transparent probability, in homage to Alan Turing — born this June in 1912 — whose Bayesian methods broke Enigma at Bletchley. The Oracle is the machine; you're the human in the loop.

Google AI. Scouting reports are generated per team; in the deployed build the call routes to the Gemini API via Cloud Functions (using cached Firestore reports for instant loads, with graceful client-side fallback).
